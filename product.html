Why we age

FAQs

1. What is NAD+ ? + x

Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD+) is a coenzyme found in every living cell. It’s essential to every cell but especially to those with mitochondria. That’s because the mitochondria is “the powerhouse of the cell.” Meaning that like any other battery or energy resource, the mitochondria depend on an external power source to keep them charged. NAD+ is that source. When our NAD+ levels are high, our cells can function at their best - so we can too. Our cells naturally produce NAD+. As we age, however, NAD+ levels decline.
